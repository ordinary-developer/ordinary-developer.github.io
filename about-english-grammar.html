About English grammar

This small post must be considered more as a reflection than a finished thought.

Once I met the next phrase in the "Learning Python" (5th edition book) by Mark Lutz: "this makes for less cluttered error messages in applications that convert between exception types while processing exception chains."

Here "exception types" means types of exceptions (a plural form).

English grammar allows the next constructions:

  • types of exception - exception types;
  • types of exceptions - exception types.

So we must understand from the context (As it is often happening in the English language).

And for example, I want to name a method "get_exception_type_names". What does it return? Names of a single type of a single exception? Names of types of exceptions? Or something else?

And again...

So we must understand from the context (As is if often happening in programming).
